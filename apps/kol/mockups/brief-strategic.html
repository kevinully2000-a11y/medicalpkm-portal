Executive Summary

Dr. John M. Flack is one of the foremost authorities on hypertension in the United States, with particular expertise in resistant hypertension and racial/ethnic disparities in cardiovascular outcomes. As Chair of the Department of Hypertensive Diseases at Virginia Commonwealth University Health, he leads a program uniquely dedicated to hypertension as a standalone clinical and research discipline. His h-index of approximately 90 and 329+ PubMed-indexed publications place him among the most-cited hypertension researchers globally. He served as President of the International Society on Hypertension in Blacks (ISHIB, 2016–2018) and contributed to the 2017 ACC/AHA Hypertension Guidelines that redefined blood pressure thresholds.

Professional Background

Dr. Flack completed his MD at the University of Michigan Medical School and MPH from the University of Michigan School of Public Health. He held faculty positions at Bowman Gray School of Medicine (Wake Forest) and served as Chief of the Division of Translational Research and Clinical Epidemiology at Wayne State University before joining VCU in 2017 as founding Chair of the Department of Hypertensive Diseases.

Expertise & Research

Dr. Flack’s research centers on three interconnected domains: (1) resistant and treatment-resistant hypertension, including combination therapy optimization and aldosterone-mediated mechanisms; (2) racial/ethnic disparities in blood pressure control, with landmark studies on early-onset hypertension in Black Americans and target organ damage trajectories; and (3) blood pressure variability as an independent cardiovascular risk factor. He has served as PI or co-PI on multiple NIH-funded studies examining these themes, and his work informed both the JNC-7 and 2017 ACC/AHA guideline panels.

Recent Work & Trends (2024–2025)

In 2024–2025, Dr. Flack has focused on the BaxHTN clinical trial program (baxdrostat, CinCor Pharma / AstraZeneca), a first-in-class aldosterone synthase inhibitor for treatment-resistant hypertension. He presented Phase 2 results at AHA 2024 showing 10–12 mmHg systolic reduction in resistant patients. He also published a key Hypertension editorial (Jan 2025) on redefining resistant hypertension classification, and continues advocacy work on hypertension screening disparities through his ISHIB leadership network.
